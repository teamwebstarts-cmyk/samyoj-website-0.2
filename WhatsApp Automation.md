### 3.1 Shared Team Inbox

**Headline:** One WhatsApp inbox. One clear owner for every conversation.

**Description:** Give sales, support, and operations teams a shared, real-time view of every customer chat. Assign conversations to the right person, see who is available, leave private notes, and keep the entire history in one place—so customers never need to repeat themselves.

**What users can do:**

- Receive and send WhatsApp messages in real time.
- Handle text, templates, images, documents, audio, video, locations, contacts, and interactive messages.
- Start a new conversation from the workspace.
- Assign or reassign a conversation to a teammate.
- See unread counts, open/pending/closed status, and the current assignee.
- Snooze a conversation and bring it back when it is time to follow up.
- Add internal notes that stay visible only to the team.
- Pause or resume AI replies for a single conversation.
- View the customer profile, labels, messages, calls, orders, and ticket context alongside the chat.
- Work within WhatsApp’s 24-hour customer-service window, with a clear prompt to use a template when needed.

**Small-card copy:**

**Every conversation has context**  
Messages, customer details, labels, internal notes, tickets, orders, and ownership stay connected—right where your team replies.

### 3.3 Visual Automation Builder

**Headline:** Build customer journeys that keep moving—even when your team is offline.

**Description:** Create visual, multi-step WhatsApp journeys for welcome messages, lead capture, campaign follow-up, after-hours support, and more. Start from a template or build your own path, then preview, test, publish, and improve it without guessing what the customer will see.

**What users can do:**

- Trigger flows when a customer sends their first message, uses a keyword, sends any message, contacts you during or outside business hours, matches a tag/segment, misses a call, replies to a campaign, or completes a call.
- Send text, approved templates, images, videos, audio, documents, locations, and contact cards.

- Send customer data to another application or CRM through a webhook step.
- A/B split a journey to compare paths.
- Create different paths by customer language.
- Validate the flow, simulate it safely, preview it as a WhatsApp conversation, save versions, and restore a previous version.
- Review starts, completions, handoffs, and step-level drop-offs.

**From “hello” to handoff, automatically**

## 8. Public-Claim Guardrails

These boundaries keep the landing page accurate to the current repository.

- Say **“built for the WhatsApp Business Cloud API”**; do not claim Meta partnership, certification, or official-business status unless separately verified.
- Say **“delivery and read tracking”** rather than promising delivery or engagement outcomes.
- Say **“AI-assisted responses with human handoff”** rather than implying the bot is always correct or fully autonomous.
- Say **“consent-aware campaigns”** rather than “fully compliant,” because compliance also depends on each customer’s lawful collection and use of data.
- Describe calling, commerce, public API, integrations, and Content Studio as **available on eligible plans/workspaces** where relevant; these capabilities are feature-flagged.
- Do **not** publicly promise AI image, video, or voice generation yet. Their current background workers create placeholder assets and are not production generation integrations.
- Do **not** claim native Google Sheets sync or a native Zapier app. The current implementation provides a Google Sheets-compatible CSV export and integration/API surfaces.
